Photo Stories is a concept feature for Facebook. It is a new feed which replaces the news feed and converts status updates, check-ins, and photo uploads into beautiful stories. This is to bring travellers, photographers and people who love Instagram to Facebook.

Step 1

Introduction

For introduction, a tooltip is shown to the users who are currently on the news feed following these rules:

  1. The tooltip is shown with an overlay over the content. Tapping on either the tooltip or the overlay dismisses the tooltip.
  2. Long pressing the news feed icon brings up a menu (slide in from bottom), replacing the icon with a cross button.
  3. Tapping the "Photo Stories" menu item takes the user to the "Photo Stories" feed.

The same process is repeated if the user wants to switch back to the news feed.

Step 2

Photo Story Feed

Photo Stories feed shows a concise version of actual photo stories. It consists of a cover image, a horizontally scrollable section of all the photos/videos and an action bar.

The name of the person is shown on top of the cover image, along with the time of the last update and the first status/check-in message of the story.

Clicking on the cover photo takes the user to the photo story detail. Clicking on the photos below will open the default fullscreen photo view.

Step 3

Photo Story Detail

Everything except the cover image fades away when transitioning from feed to the detailed story. The cover image then repositions to its new position in the story and the remaining information fades in sequentially.

A summary of initial location used in the check-in is pulled from Wikipedia and displayed at the top of story. Apart from that, the photos are also arranged smartly. The photos with the most likes take up the whole row while others are divided into two/three columns.
